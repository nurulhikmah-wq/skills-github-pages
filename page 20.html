Physics Problems

Problem 1

A 5 kg object is acted upon by a force of 10 N. Calculate the acceleration experienced by the object.

Solution

We can use Newton's second law of motion to solve this problem:

$$a = \frac{F}{m}$$

Where:

  • a is acceleration
  • F is force
  • m is mass

Plugging in the given values:

$$a = \frac{10 \, \text{N}}{5 \, \text{kg}} = 2 \, \text{m/s}^2$$

So, the acceleration experienced by the object is 2 m/s².

Problem 2

A 5 kg object is dropped from a height of 10 m. Calculate the time it takes for the object to hit the ground and its velocity upon impact. (Ignore air resistance.)

Solution

We can use the kinematic equations of motion to solve this problem. First, we calculate the time (t) it takes for the object to hit the ground:

$$h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

Where:

  • h is the height (10 m)
  • g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)

Rearranging to solve for t:

$$t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$$

Plugging in the given values:

$$t = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 10 \, \text{m}}{9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2}} \approx 1.43 \, \text{s}$$

So, the time it takes for the object to hit the ground is approximately 1.43 seconds.

Next, we calculate the velocity (v) upon impact:

$$v = gt$$

Plugging in the values:

$$v = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2 \times 1.43 \, \text{s} \approx 14 \, \text{m/s}$$

So, the velocity upon impact is approximately 14 m/s.
